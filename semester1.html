An Astrologer’s Day – Complete MCQs

About the Author

R. K. Narayan (1906–2001) was one of the most prominent Indian writers in English and a pioneer of Indian English fiction. He is best known for creating the fictional town of Malgudi, which serves as the setting for many of his novels and short stories. Narayan’s writing is marked by simplicity, gentle humor, irony, and deep insight into human nature. He focused mainly on ordinary people and everyday situations, presenting Indian life with warmth and realism. His major works include Swami and Friends, The Bachelor of Arts, The English Teacher, and the short story An Astrologer’s Day. He was widely admired both in India and abroad for making Indian experiences accessible to a global audience.

About the Text

An Astrologer’s Day is a short story by R. K. Narayan that explores themes of fate, coincidence, irony, guilt, and human psychology. The story revolves around an astrologer who earns his living not through real astrological knowledge but through clever observation and experience. Set in a busy marketplace, the narrative takes a dramatic turn when the astrologer encounters a stranger who turns out to be a man from his violent past. Through this unexpected meeting, Narayan highlights how past actions resurface in surprising ways. The story also subtly criticizes blind faith in astrology while portraying the resilience and intelligence of common people. The ending brings a sense of relief and irony, making the story both engaging and thought-provoking.
